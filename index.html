About

I am a graduate research assistant at The University of Texas in Austin. I collaborate with Dr. Hans Hofmann to study functional variability of single neurons and to enhance transdiciplinary training in data intensive approaches.

• My research aims to better characterize variability in neuromolecular mechanisms regulating diverse behaviors, especially social behaviors • My educational efforts aim to transform graduate training in computational approaches to biological questions • My outreach activities are tailored to promote the sharing of ideas across typical disciplinary boundaries

Teaching

I was an organic chemistry lab teaching specialists full-time from 2006-2009. In 2012 I was a teaching assistant for Problems in Modern Biology in with Dr. Ruth Buskirk.

In the summers, I am a course developer for the Neural Systems & Behavior at the Marine Biological Laboratory. My mission is to conduct integrative research and develop new educational exercises to enhance the course curricula. Here's the molecular lab manual I created for teaching neural gene expression profiling techniques. Event Organization

I love bringing people together to talk science, especially when I see that these interactions foster new relationships and projects. Working with the The Center for Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, I have many opportunities to organize networking events, yearly symposia, and training opportunities for researchers using computational approaches to solve biological problems.
