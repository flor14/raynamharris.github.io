About

I am a graduate research assistant at The University of Texas in Austin. I collaborate with Dr. Hans Hofmann to study functional variability of single neurons and to enhance transdiciplinary training in interdisciplinary approaches to research. My research focuses on on understanding transcriptional variability in neurons with single neuron resolution. My educational efforts aim to improve training in computational approaches for bench scientists. My outreach activities promote the sharing of ideas across typical disciplinary boundaries.

    • Teaching

    In 2015, I became a certified Software Carpentry Instructor and have since taught UNIX to a wide audience of aspiring bioinformatians. In 2016, I became a certified Software Carpentry Instructor Trainer, which means that I teach people how to teach Software Carpentry Workshops. I also serve on the Steering Committee as well as the Mentoring and the Assessment Subcommittees. I have learned more about teaching and bioinformatics from the Software Carpentry community than through any formal education.

    In the summers, I am a course developer for the Neural Systems & Behavior at the Marine Biological Laboratory. My mission is to conduct integrative research and develop new educational exercises to enhance the course curricula. Here's the molecular lab manual I created for teaching neural gene expression profiling techniques. Here's a 30 minute video of me giving a lecture titled Time and money: Techniques for neural gene expression profiling.

    I was an organic chemistry lab teaching specialists full-time from 2006-2009. In 2012 I was a teaching assistant for Problems in Modern Biology in with Dr. Ruth Buskirk.

    Community

    I love bringing people together to talk science, especially when I see that these interactions foster new relationships and projects. Working with the The Center for Computational Biology and Bioinformatics, I have many opportunities to organize networking events, yearly symposia, and training opportunities for researchers using computational approaches to solve biological problems.

    Hablando en español

    Para aprender español, tomé algunos cursos en la escuela y un curso intensivo en el Ecuador. En 2008 fui a Costa Rica hacer la investigación científica en el Instituto Nacional de Biodiversidad. Allí, trabajé en el campo y el laboratorio. Todos los meses, fui con dos compañeros de trabajo en el Caribe o del Pacífico para bucear y recoger muestras de sedimento. Luego regresamos al laboratorio para crecer y examinar la micro-hongos que viven en el sedimento. Aprendí mucho y tenía una experiencia increíble. Al año siguiente, cuando volví a Austin, enseñé clases de español a los niños en la escuela primaria. De vez en cuando, traduzco documentos del Inglés al español o viceversa y chat en español con amigos a través de cervezas o copas de vino.
